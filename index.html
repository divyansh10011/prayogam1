January 2026 Kimsuky Quishing Campaign

The Threat: The FBI warned that North Korean Kimsuky operators were embedding malicious QR codes inside spearphishing lures to push victims from managed laptops onto mobile devices, then redirecting them to fake Microsoft 365, Okta, and VPN login pages built for credential theft and session hijacking.

Mobile Redirect Trap
Session Token Theft

02 HIGH

February 2026 Edge Device Intrusion Wave

The Threat: CISA, FBI, and NCSC warned that nation-state actors were actively exploiting unsupported firewalls, VPN gateways, routers, and load balancers at the network edge to gain initial access, maintain persistence, and pivot deeper into modern environments that still looked healthy from the inside.

Unsupported VPN Gateway
Internet-Facing Exposure

LIVE EVIDENCE: CISA/FBI/NCSC fact sheet, February 5, 2026. EOS edge devices called out as active entry points for nation-state compromise.

03 HIGH

January 2026 OT Remote Access Pivot

The Threat: New international OT guidance in January 2026 focused on a familiar failure: insecure remote access paths between IT and OT. Flat trust relationships, shared credentials, and weak segmentation let attackers jump from an internet-facing foothold into operational systems that control physical processes.

Flat Trust Boundary
Shared Admin Credentials

LIVE EVIDENCE: Secure Connectivity Principles for OT, January 14, 2026. Guidance highlights remote-access pathways and segmentation failures as high-risk OT exposure.

04 HIGH

February 2026 WhatsApp Worm Stealer

The Threat: Microsoft documented a campaign where WhatsApp abuse was used to spread Eternidade Stealer. Hijacked accounts sent malicious attachments to every contact, while the payload monitored banking portals, payment services, crypto wallets, and financial sessions to steal credentials at scale.

Hijacked Chat Account
Financial Session Theft

LIVE EVIDENCE: Microsoft Security Blog, February 2, 2026. WhatsApp abuse and Eternidade delivery observed in late-2025 activity still shaping 2026 defenses.

05 CRITICAL

February 2026 Crystal PDF Malvertising

The Threat: Attackers pushed a fake PDF editor called Crystal PDF through Google Ads, malvertising, and SEO poisoning. Once launched, it created scheduled-task persistence and quietly harvested browser cookies, session data, and credential caches from Chrome and Firefox.

SEO Poisoning
Browser Cookie Theft

LIVE EVIDENCE: Microsoft Security Blog, February 2, 2026. CrystalPDF.exe detailed as a browser-focused infostealer delivered by ad poisoning.

06 HIGH

February 2026 ATM Jackpotting Surge

The Threat: The FBI reported a sharp increase in malware-enabled ATM jackpotting, with attackers physically opening machines, replacing or tampering with drives, and deploying Ploutus-family malware that talks directly to the XFS layer to force unauthorized cash dispensing in minutes.

Ploutus Malware
XFS Command Abuse

LIVE EVIDENCE: FBI FLASH, February 19, 2026. More than 700 U.S. incidents in 2025 alone were tied to this cash-out technique.
